<section id="curriculum-details" class="curriculum content-area-yellow">
<div class="max-width has-padding">
<header class="section-header">
<h1>We Embrace The Details</h1>
</header>
<div id="curriculum">
<h3>Unit 1: The Basics of Data Science</h3>
<div class="unit">
<h4>Introduction to Data Exploration</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the data mining workflow and the key traits of a successful data scientist.</li>
<li>Extract, format, and preprocess data using UNIX command-line tools.</li>
<li>Explore &amp; visualize data using R and ggplot2.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Introduction to Machine Learning</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Explain the concepts and applications of supervised &amp; unsupervised learning techniques.</li>
<li>Describe categorical and continuous feature spaces, including examples and techniques for each.</li>
<li>Discuss the purpose of machine learning and the interpretation of predictive modeling results.</li>
<li>Create a predictive model using linear regression.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<h3>Unit 2: Fundamental Modeling Techniques</h3>
<div class="unit">
<h4>K-Nearest Neighbors Classification</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the setting and goal of a classification task.</li>
<li>Minimize prediction error using training &amp; test sets, optimize predictive performance using cross-validation.</li>
<li>Understand the kNN classification algorithm, its intuition and implementation.</li>
<li>Implement the "hello world" of machine learning (kNN classification of iris dataset).</li>
</ul>
<h4>Naive Bayes Classification</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Outline the basic principles of probability, including conditional probability and Bayes theorem.</li>
<li>Describe inference in the Bayesian setting, including the prior and posterior distributions and the likelihood function.</li>
<li>Understand the naive Bayes classifier and its assumptions.</li>
<li>Implement a spam filter using the naive Bayes technique.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Regression &amp; Regularization</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Explain the concepts of regression models, including their assumptions and applications.</li>
<li>Discuss the motivation for regularization techniques and their use.</li>
<li>Implement a regularized fit.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Logistic Regression</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the applications of logistic regression to classification problems and probability estimation.</li>
<li>Introduce the concepts underlying logistic regression, including its relation to other regression models.</li>
<li>Predict the probability of a user action on a website using logistic regression.</li>
</ul>
<h4>K-Means Clustering</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Explain the purpose of exploratory data analysis, its applications in continuous and categorical feature spaces, and the interpretation and use of clustering results.</li>
<li>Discuss the importance of the distance function in cluster formation, as well as the importance of scale normalization.</li>
<li>Implement a k-means clustering algorithm.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3>Unit 3: Further Modeling Techniques</h3>
<div class="unit">
<h4>Machine Learning in Python</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Introduce Python and its usefulness for data analysis tasks.</li>
<li>Experiment with scikit-learn, a general-purpose machine learning library for Python.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Ensemble Techniques</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe general ensemble techniques such as bagging and boosting.</li>
<li>Build an enhanced classification algorithm using AdaBoost.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Decision Trees &amp; Random Forests</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the use and construction of decision trees for classification tasks.</li>
<li>Create a random forest model for ensemble classification.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Support Vector Machines</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the motivation for non-linear classification techniques, as well as the conceptual basis for their use.</li>
<li>Understand the advantages and disadvantages of black box models.</li>
<li>Implement a non-linear classifier &amp; compare results with linear classification.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Dimensionality Reduction</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Explain the practical and conceptual difficulties in working with very high-dimensional data.</li>
<li>Understand the application and use of dimensionality reduction techniques.</li>
<li>Draw inferences from high-dimensional datasets using principal components analysis.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Recommendation Systems</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Explain the use of recommendation systems, and discuss several familiar examples.</li>
<li>Understand the underlying concepts, including collaborative &amp; content-based filtering.</li>
<li>Implement a recommendation system.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3>Unit 4: Other Tools</h3>
<div class="unit">
<h4>Database Techniques</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Introduce concepts and use of relational databases, alternative database technologies such as NoSQL, and popular examples of each.</li>
<li>Interact with a relational db (MySQL) and a NoSQL db (MongoDB), compare and contrast their use.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Social Network Analysis</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the use of graphs and graph theory to analyze problems in network analysis.</li>
<li>Explore network visualization with Gephi.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Map-Reduce</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Describe the concepts of parallel computing and applications to problems in big data.</li>
<li>Introduce the map-reduce framework and popular implementations including Hadoop.</li>
<li>Implement and explore examples of map-reduce tasks.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Where to Go Next</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Review of concepts and examples from preceding weeks.</li>
<li>Discussion of resources &amp; tools for further study.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Final Project Presentations</h4>
<ul class="curriculum-block">
<li>Students will have various options for their final project with the objective of solving a business problem using a either a pre-determined data set or a data set of their own.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>

<section class="course-specific-faq content-area" id="faq">
<header class="section-header max-width has-padding">
<h1>Get Answers</h1>
<p class="subhead">We love questions, almost as much as we love providing answers. Here are a few samplings of what we're typically asked, along with our responses:</p>
</header>
<div class="max-width has-padding">
<div class="grid-row sidebar-right">
<div class="column"> <p class="faq-question">Q: Why is this course relevant today?</p>
<p>Given the prevalence of technologies and the amount of data available in the online world about users, products, and the content that we generate, businesses can be making so much more well-informed decisions if this vast amount of data was more deeply analyzed through the use of data science. The data science course provides the tools, methods, and practical experience to enable you to make accurate predictions about data, which ultimately leads to better decision-making in business, and the use of smarter technology (think recommendation systems or targeted ads).</p>
<br />

<p class="faq-question">Q: What practical skill sets can I expect to have upon completion of the course?</p>
<p>This course will provide you with technical skills in machine learning, algorithms, and data modeling which will allow you to make accurate predictions about your data. You will be creating your models using R and Python so you will gain a good grasp of these two programming languages. Furthermore, you will learn how to parse and clean your data which can take up to 70% of your time as a data scientist.</p>
<br />

<p class="faq-question">Q: Who will I be sitting next to in this course?</p>
<p>Individuals who have a strong interest in manipulating large data sets, finding patterns in data, and making predictions.</p>
<p>Software developers who want to solve problems that involve large data sets, such as predicting user behavior on their website, making decisions, or the best way to classify content.</p>
<p>Individuals with a good grasp of programming, a solid knowledge of statistics and probability but missing the intersection of them both.</p>
<br />

<p class="faq-question">Q: Are there any prerequisites?</p>
<ul>
<li>A good grasp of college-level statistics and probability.</li>
<li>Ability to program in a scripting language such as Python or R.</li>
</ul>
